Group Members - RizalWiki (Group - 3)

Members of the Rizal Study Group

This page showcases the members of Group 3 who worked together on the RizalWiki project. Each member contributed to researching, writing, and compiling information about Jose Rizal's life, family, and legacy. Below is a detailed overview of each group member and their contributions.[1]

Alice Smith serves as the project leader for Group 3's RizalWiki assignment. She coordinated the research efforts and ensured that all sections were completed on time.

Background

Alice is a fourth-year student majoring in History at the university. Her interest in Philippine history, particularly the national hero Jose Rizal, began during her sophomore year when she took a course on Southeast Asian history.

Contributions to the Project

As project leader, Alice was responsible for:

  • Coordinating team meetings and delegating tasks
  • Reviewing and editing all content for accuracy
  • Ensuring proper citation and reference formatting
  • Final quality check before submission

Bob Johnson

Bob Johnson
Bob Johnson - Group Member
Role
Researcher

Bob Johnson was primarily responsible for researching Jose Rizal's early education and family background. His meticulous research provided valuable insights into Rizal's childhood and formative years.

Background

Bob is a third-year student with a keen interest in educational history. He has always been fascinated by how historical figures' early lives shaped their future accomplishments.

Contributions to the Project

Bob's main contributions included:

  • Research on Rizal's early education in Calamba and Biñan
  • Investigation of Rizal's parents - Francisco Mercado and Teodora Alonso
  • Documentation of Rizal's siblings and their roles
  • Collection of primary sources from various archives

Charlie Brown

Charlie Brown
Charlie Brown - Group Member
Role
Content Writer

Charlie Brown was responsible for writing and organizing the content for the RizalWiki project. His excellent writing skills helped present complex historical information in an accessible manner.

Background

Charlie is a fourth-year English major with a passion for creative writing. He believes that history comes alive through well-crafted narratives.

Contributions to the Project

Charlie's main contributions included:

  • Writing the Identity section of the article
  • Creating engaging lead paragraphs
  • Editing and proofreading all sections
  • Ensuring consistent tone and style throughout

Diana Prince

Diana Prince
Diana Prince - Group Member
Role
Web Designer

Diana Prince was responsible for the web design and technical implementation of the RizalWiki project. She created the Wikipedia-style template and ensured the page was visually appealing and user-friendly.

Diana is a third-year Computer Science student with a minor in Design. She has always been interested in combining technology with creative expression.

  • Implementing the infobox system
  • Adding responsive design for mobile devices
  • Integrating the table of contents

Ethan Hunt

Ethan Hunt
Ethan Hunt - Group Member
Role
Fact Checker

Ethan Hunt served as the fact-checker for the group, ensuring all information was accurate and properly cited. His attention to detail helped maintain the academic integrity of the project.

Background

Ethan is a fourth-year student majoring in Journalism. His training in investigative reporting has made him meticulous about verifying facts and sources.

Contributions to the Project

Ethan's main contributions included:

  • Fact-checking all historical claims
  • Verifying dates, names, and events
  • Compiling and organizing references

  • Reviewing the Myths and Misconceptions section
